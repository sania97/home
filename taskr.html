UNDERSTANDING THE PROBLEM

With any co living situation, there is bound to be conflict. One of main sources of conflict is related to shared tasks, such as housework. Often times, certain members of a group will feel like they are doing the brunt of the work in a shared space, while others mean well but just need to be reminded. This app was designed to bridge that gap by facilitating communication.  Other problems relating to communication include the lack of actual time to communicate. Vastly different schedules reinforce that problem. An app that members of a group can access can aid in those specific issues.  

THE USER

My first stage of brainstorming was to understand the user. I asked myself the 5Ws (Who What When Where Why) to better empathize with the user and outline my goals of the app.

Who At first I thought of my own life where this type of app would be useful. So the users I designed for were roommate groups, and family residences.  Later I realized the app would also be useful in office settings, or even for a single person with ADHD/ADD, who needs their tasks written out
What Shared tasks are created in the app and then distributed equally
Where the shared space, as the goal is to keep that space as conflict free as possible
When App can be used whenever, however the effects will be produced whenever in that shared space
Why The goals are to create an avenue for communication and alleviate conflict

UX PROCESS

RESEARCH

Competitive Analysis



I searched for chore apps and found that many were family based, and had lots of features that made them feel juvenile. I decided my design would appeal to as many people as possible, with a more modern look. That would also mean that users should be able to pick their specifics.  Also, none had a opportunity to conduct an equitable assignment of tasks, a feature that I think helps make my product extremely useful.

Interviews



I conducted 10 in person, short interviews with potential users of my product. I asked them about their feelings toward chores, and how chores were kept track of in their house. I used these to create user personas.

IDEATION

User Personas



I used my interviews from the Research phase of the project to create four User Personas. Referring to these aided me in my design. Each user was a manifestation of a potential user of TASKR, and what their goals and frustrations were.

WIREFRAME

Sketches



I used pen and paper to sketch out the many frames I would make. ideas, sketches, brainstorming, what worked and what didn’t, testing results, iterations





During this process, I figured out that two features would need to be the focal point: making a group, and making an activity. Those features would ultimately lead to the Group page, which would feature a calendar for reference.

DESIGN & PROTOTYPE

Design, test/feedback, iterations, final design



I referred to my wireframes and FIGMA to create hi-fidelity designs.

Testing and Feedback



I asked four acquaintances, who matched the user persona profiles as closely as possible, to try out the prototype. From this I was able to fix some features that needed refining, such as font colors and button sizes.

I also chose to use houseplants as a design inspiration, promote a feeling of home life and accomplishment. So the main color of the app is green. One of my favorite features in the app is the garden, which I decided will grow based on if members of the group finish their tasks. This is another feature that promotes a sense of accomplishment. CONCLUSION



This was my first, fully designed app. In the future, I would love to work with a developer to code it and put it out on the market, as I feel like it would be very useful for many people in the world, including me! In this particular project, I learned that user testing is so very important, as it helped me single out design features that could be improved. For my next project, I think I will wireframe a bit more thoroughly. I will start with pen and paper at first, but then add a stage for lo fidelity mockups.
